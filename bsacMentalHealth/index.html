Our Vision

We aim to establish mental health awareness through advocacy, support, and education. This can become a reality by enhancing the school curriculum, through the implementation of yearly health classes. This class would allow students to gain insight toward the issue, as they would be introduced to the evolution of this growing issue. In addition, we hope to change D.O.E policy by mandating a minimum of two guest speakers a year. In hopes that providing the student body with someone they can sympathize with will illustrate that they are not alone and encourage them to speak up.


Why does it keep growing?

The feeling of sadness, worthlessness, and restlessness has caused a huge impact to our youth. With the rise of social media, which enables one to be judged and ridiculed by others has been linked to the spread of mental illness issues.

In simple terms, it can be caused by many factors such as

  • -Familial issues
  • -Bullying
  • -Chronic illnesses or other physical conditions
  • -Untreated mental or substance-abuse problems
  • -Lack of coping skills

What has changed?
  • -Dramatic increase in violent tv shows, movies and video games.
  • -Social media pressure
  • -Being exposed to a multitude of opinions (on tv & online)
  • -Gender confusion
  • -Early access to, and the acceptance of illegal drugs
  • -Reduced face to face interactions and social support

Society is changing.

The mental health of our generation is slowly depreciating and it will only get worse as time goes on. Society is ignoring the important signs as they believe that they can help themselves, because of their beliefs that nothing is wrong with them. This fixed mindset can not continue. In order to combat this growing issue, the school community needs to enhance the curriculum and invest more time into the wellbeing of the students. When teenagers take matters into their own hands their solution is self-harm, such as cutting themselves, Bulimia (binging & purging), and even taking their life.
